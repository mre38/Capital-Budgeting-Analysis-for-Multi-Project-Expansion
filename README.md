
#  Project Title: Capital Budgeting Analysis for Hotel Expansion Projects

Orion Hospitality Group is planning to expand its operations and has limited capital to invest. The company must choose between three major investment projects but cannot fund all of them due to budget constraints. 

**Management objectives:**
* Which project will generate the highest returns?
* Which project is the least risky?
* Which investment will maximize shareholder value?

## Objective
The goal of this analysis was to evaluate multiple investment projects using rigorous financial techniques. The study compares profitability using:
* **Net Present Value (NPV)**
* **Internal Rate of Return (IRR)**
* **Payback Period**

The final output provides a data-driven selection and clear investment recommendations.

---

## Project Options & Assumptions

###  Project A: Luxury Hotel (7 Years)
* **Initial Investment:** $5,000,000
* **Revenue:** $2,600,000
* **Discount Rate:** 10%
* **Revenue Growth:** 8% Per Year
* **Variable Costs:** 50% of revenue
* **Fixed Costs:** $500,000 (constant)

###  Project B: Restaurant Chain (5 Years)
* **Initial Investment:** $2,500,000
* **Revenue:** $1,600,000
* **Discount Rate:** 10%
* **Revenue Growth:** 12% Per Year
* **Variable Costs:** 55% of revenue
* **Fixed Costs:** $300,000 (constant)

###  Project C: Event Center (4 Years)
* **Initial Investment:** $1,500,000
* **Revenue:** $1,300,000
* **Discount Rate:** 10%
* **Revenue Growth:** 15% Per Year
* **Variable Costs:** 60% of revenue
* **Fixed Costs:** $200,000 (constant)

## Key Analysis Questions & Data Insights

#### Which project will generate the highest returns and maximize shareholder value?**
 **Project A (Luxury Hotel)** is the only viable option for maximizing shareholder value. It is the only project with a **positive NPV ($400,706)**. In capital budgeting, a positive NPV indicates that the project’s returns exceed the cost of capital, thereby adding direct value to the firm. Projects B and C both have negative NPVs, meaning they would result in a net loss when adjusted for the time value of money

#### Which project is the least risky?**
 **Project C (Event Center)** appears the least risky because it has the shortest **Payback Period (3.5 years)**. However, since Project C fails to meet the required 10% discount rate (returning only 6.99%), the speed of repayment does not compensate for the overall loss in value. Therefore, Project A is the safest long-term bet because its **IRR (12.13%)** safely exceeds its 10% discount rate

---

##  Final Investment Recommendation
The analysis concludes that **Project A (Luxury Hotel)** is the only recommended investment. While it requires the largest capital outlay, it is the only project that clears its hurdle rate and generates a positive return for the company

##  Technical Features of the Analysis
* **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF):** Modeled annual inflows and outflows to determine the present value of future earnings.
* **Hurdle Rate Testing:** Compared the Internal Rate of Return (IRR) against project-specific discount rates to filter out value-destructive investments.
* **Time-to-Recovery Tracking:** Calculated the Payback Period to assess how quickly the initial capital investment is recouped.
